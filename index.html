My Biography

Learn more about my journey, passions, and aspirations in the field of Finance and business, engineering, and STEM education.

Early Life

My name is Milimo Siabana. I was born on July 11, 2003, in Maakula Village, Sinazongwe District, in Zambia's Southern Province. My childhood was shaped by the rhythms of rural life: the vastness of Lake Kariba, the responsibility of tending cattle, and the creativity born from necessity.

My early years were spent fishing for bream with friends, farming, and looking after our cattle. These weren't just pastimes; they were a means of subsistence. I learned the value of perseverance on days when the fish weren't biting, and the importance of resourcefulness by selling my catch to buy clothes and support my grandmother, who raised me. From molding animals out of clay and crafting wire vehicles, I developed a hands-on creativity that would later become the foundation for my passion in STEM.

Life was not without its challenges or dangers. I recall a harrowing moment when my brothers and I were nearly attacked by crocodiles while playing near Lake Kariba. I walked barefoot for most of my youth, mending worn-out flip-flops because my family's limited income was prioritized for food. Yet, these experiences didn't harden me; they taught me resilience, ingenuity, and a deep appreciation for every opportunity.

The Reluctant Scholar: A Spark Ignited

I enrolled in Grade 1 at Sinanjola Basic School in 2012, two years later than most, due to our financial constraints. Initially, my heart was still at the lake, and I frequently dodged school for the first two terms. Astonishingly, despite my absences, I still managed to finish second in my class. My teacher, Mrs. Manyanina Chimpati, showed immense patience. A pivotal moment came when she used my name as an example on the board, helping me overcome my confusion with letters - I used to write W instead of M when writing my first name ' Milimo'. Another turning point was scoring 2/10 on a math exercise. That jolted me into action. In Term 3, I committed fully to my studies, never missed a lesson, and finished the year as the top student. The motivation of receiving GRZ books and pencils for the top 3 students fueled my competitive spirit, and I fiercely maintained my top position throughout primary school.

My academic journey was marked by early competitions and painful lessons. In Grade 2, I failed to advance in a spelling bee after misspelling "trousers", a disappointment that stung deeply as it meant missing a trip to some new places. However, I also discovered a love for science and mathematics. In Grade 6, a dedicated teacher, Mr. Phiri, introduced me to the concept of national technical schools like Hillcrest. He lit a fire in me, telling me that scoring above 800 in my Grade 7 exams could be my ticket. From that moment, my momentum shifted irrevocably.

The Rise of a STEM Mind

I thrived in leadership roles, serving as Prefect in charge of the Production Unit, where I managed the school garden with pride. My passion for academics found an outlet in competitions. I represented my school in SOSTAZ, presenting on Zambian national symbols and reaching the district level. But it was the JETS (Junior Engineers, Technicians, and Scientists) competitions that truly changed my trajectory.

I participated in Mathematics Olympiads and quizzes, succeeding all the way to the National JETS Fair in Kitwe. This was my first time traveling outside my province, and though I didn't win, the exposure was transformative. It connected my innate curiosity to the real-world possibilities of STEM. This drive culminated in a Grade 7 score of 821 out of 900—a testament to my relentless effort.

Overcoming Barriers: Forging My Own Path

Despite my achievements, financial barriers remained daunting. I was initially selected for Zimba Secondary School but couldn't attend due to the costs. Undeterred, I took initiative. I requested a piece of land from my grandmother, grew cotton, and raised the money to pay for my Grade 8 fees. I even volunteered to water the school garden during holidays to cover my tuition. My resourcefulness extended to my studies; I devoured books from the school library and borrowed from teachers.

My perseverance paid off. In Grade 9, I made history at Sinanjola Basic School by scoring 534 marks out of 600, shattering the previous record. This achievement earned me a place at Edgar Chagwa Lungu National STEM School. While financial constraints once again threatened to block my path, providence intervened. I was sponsored by SESTUZ to attend the prestigious Hillcrest National STEM Secondary School for my Grades 10-12.

Excelling on a National Stage

At Hillcrest, I seized every opportunity. I consistently received the Best Student award from Grades 10 to 12 and served with passion as the Olympiad and Quiz Coordinator. In my final year, I was honored to be selected as Head Boy, leading our cohort to achieve over 100 six-point scores at Grade 12.

My competitive drive continued. I progressed through district and provincial levels in mathematics competitions, and in 2023, I achieved a crowning glory: I won the National Senior Mathematics Olympiad in Lusaka. I graduated from Grade 12 with a perfect score of six points, with 1s in all eight subjects I took.

My academic journey continued to soar when I was awarded an A-Level scholarship by First Quantum Minerals Limited to study at Trident College in Solwezi. There, I continued to excel, serving as Head of House, participating in community service, and winning numerous awards. I graduated in 2025 as the Dux Scholar of the Class of 2025, with an impressive average of 94.13%, and top marks in Further Mathematics (97%) and Chemistry (94%).

From a barefoot boy fishing in Lake Kariba to a national scholar, my early life has been a testament to the power of resilience, resourcefulness, and an unwavering belief in the power of education.

Higher Education Loans & Scholarships Board (HELSB)

Government-funded loans and scholarships for Zambian students pursuing higher education at recognized public and private institutions.

Eligibility Criteria

  • Zambian citizen with a valid NRC
  • Admitted to an accredited higher education institution
  • Good academic record (Minimum 5 O-level credits)
  • Not benefiting from any other scholarship
  • Meets specific program requirements

Application Requirements

  • Completed HELSB application form
  • Certified copy Grade 12 Certificate or statement of results
  • Birth Certificate (Original & Photocopy)
  • Certified copy of a valid National Registration Card (NRC)
  • Admission letter from respective institution
  • Fee structure from institution
  • Recent passport-size photographs
  • Recommendation letters (School/Chief/Headman)
  • Parent/Guardian income statement
